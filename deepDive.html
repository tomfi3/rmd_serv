Question 1: Are there any other approaches you would like to suggest? If so, please tell us here.

Common Points Raised:

  • Reduction of Car Usage: Emphasis on reducing car usage within the borough by increasing parking charges and creating pedestrianized areas.
  • Improved Cycling Infrastructure: Strong call for creating separated cycle paths and dedicated bike lanes with physical barriers for safety.
  • Enforcement of Anti-Idling Laws: Importance of enforcing anti-idling regulations, especially near schools and busy intersections.
  • Addressing Aircraft Pollution: Concerns about air pollution from aircraft and suggestions to tackle noise and particulate emissions.
  • Regulating Wood Burners: Addressing pollution caused by wood and multi-fuel burners with proactive council measures.

Good Suggestions:

  • Installation of Covered Bicycle Parking: Providing free, covered bicycle parking across the borough to encourage cycling.
  • Incentives for E-Bike Purchases: Offering refunds or incentives for residents to purchase e-bikes as an alternative to cars.
  • Public Involvement in Enforcement: Allowing public reporting of idling vehicles, possibly through video evidence, to assist enforcement.
  • Evaluating Impact of Existing Programs: Assessing current initiatives like "School Streets" to ensure they effectively reduce pollution.

Issues Raised:

  • Perception of Anti-Car Bias: Some feel the plan is biased against car users and overlooks those who rely on vehicles.
  • Ineffectiveness of Current Measures: Concerns that existing measures, such as anti-idling signs, are ineffective without proper enforcement.
  • Impact of Road Closures: Road closures (e.g., Bushy Park) leading to increased traffic and idling in other areas, worsening air quality.

Question 2: Are there any aspects of the plan you think should be prioritised? If so, please tell us here.

Common Points Raised:

  • Active Travel and Cycling Infrastructure: Prioritizing development of safe, segregated cycling paths and pedestrian areas.
  • Reducing Car Dependency: Implementing measures to significantly reduce car usage, especially for short journeys and school runs.
  • Enforcement of Regulations: Enhancing enforcement of existing regulations, such as anti-idling laws.
  • Addressing Aircraft Pollution: Giving higher priority to tackling air pollution from aircraft overflying the borough.

Good Suggestions:

  • More Ambitious Targets: Setting more aggressive timelines and goals for reducing emissions and improving air quality.
  • Improving Public Transport: Enhancing bus and train services to provide viable alternatives to car use.
  • Targeting High-Polluting Vehicles: Focusing on reducing emissions from SUVs and older, more polluting vehicles.

Issues Raised:

  • Lack of Clarity and Action: Some feel the plan is high on analysis but lacks concrete actions and measurable outcomes.
  • Concerns Over Wood Burning: Desire for the plan to more strongly address pollution from wood burners.
  • Balancing Initiatives with Practicality: Ensuring initiatives do not cause other issues like increased congestion elsewhere.

Further Comments and Holistic Summary:

Overall Sentiment:

The respondents express a strong desire for more decisive and effective action to improve air quality in the borough. There is significant support for measures that reduce car dependency, enhance active travel infrastructure, and enforce existing regulations. Concerns are raised about the effectiveness of current programs and the need for better enforcement and evaluation.

Common Themes:

  • Active Travel Promotion: Need for safe, segregated cycling infrastructure and pedestrian-friendly streets.
  • Reducing Vehicle Emissions: Advocating for measures that discourage car use and target high-polluting vehicles.
  • Public Transport Improvements: Calls for enhanced public transport options as viable alternatives to driving.
  • Addressing Non-Traffic Pollution Sources: Highlighting pollution from aircraft and wood burners.
  • Enforcement and Regulation: Belief that effectiveness hinges on proper enforcement of regulations.

Good Suggestions Across Responses:

  • Community Involvement: Engaging residents in enforcement efforts and decision-making processes.
  • Learning from Successful Models: Adopting strategies from other cities that have successfully improved air quality.
  • Holistic Approach: Ensuring solutions in one area do not create problems in another.

Issues Raised Across Responses:

  • Anti-Car Perception: Some feel the plan is biased against car users and doesn't consider those who rely on vehicles.
  • Implementation Doubts: Skepticism regarding the council's ability to effectively implement the plan.
  • Data Transparency: Need for more comprehensive data and transparency in conclusions and actions.

Conclusion:

The feedback indicates strong community interest in improving air quality, with a focus on sustainable transport solutions, effective enforcement, and addressing all significant pollution sources. To enhance the plan, the council should consider these suggestions, address the concerns raised, and ensure clear communication and engagement with residents throughout the implementation process.
